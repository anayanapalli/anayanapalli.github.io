> The real power players in any system are the people trusted to make the most expensive decisions that cannot be easily undone.

<p class="lead">
  <span class="first-letter">V</span>isibility, charisma, and confidence often look like power, but they are just signals of power, not power itself.
</p>

<strong>Real power is the authority to make high-stakes decisions where being wrong can cause huge irreversible damage to the system.</strong>

Once you internalize this principle, you can go to any organization, company, government, fund or institute and easily spot where real power actually lives.

You will stop looking for signals of power and start asking its defining question:

<strong>Whose decision, if wrong, can cause huge irreversible damage to the system?</strong>

That person, role or team is the place where the system has placed its _deepest_ trust.

<strong>And trust, at scale, is what real power actually is.</strong>
